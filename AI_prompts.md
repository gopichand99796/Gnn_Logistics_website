GNN Logistics Inc. — Website Blueprint
The Voyage Protocol

I. Design Philosophy

Core Principle: The Invisible Journey

This website does not present information—it transports the visitor. Every pixel serves the narrative of cargo moving across the globe. The user never reads about logistics; they experience the passage of goods from origin to destination.

Guiding Tenets:

Motion as Meaning — Nothing moves arbitrarily. Every animation communicates distance, transfer, or transformation of cargo.

Scroll as Travel — The vertical scroll becomes horizontal distance. Scrolling down moves the shipment forward in its journey.

Continuity over Sections — Traditional "sections" dissolve into waypoints on a continuous route. The visual thread—a ship, a route line, a container—never fully disappears.

Restraint Creates Premium — Animations are deliberate, slow, weighted. Cargo has mass. Ships have inertia. Nothing snaps or bounces.

The World Moves, Not the Camera — The viewport remains stable while the world of logistics flows through it, creating the sensation of watching a journey unfold.

II. Story Narrative

The Tale of a Single Shipment

The website follows one hypothetical cargo container—Container GNN-7749—from factory floor to final doorstep.

Act I: Origin (Hero → About)
A container sits at a bustling Asian port. Cranes lift it aboard a massive vessel. The journey begins.

Act II: The Crossing (Services → Statistics)
The ship traverses the Pacific. Days pass. Weather changes. The container moves through the global supply chain's invisible infrastructure.

Act III: Landfall (Fleet → Logistics Journey)
The ship reaches port. The container is unloaded, processed, stacked, scanned. It transitions from sea to land.

Act IV: The Final Mile (Service Regions → Testimonials)
A truck carries the container across highways and through cities. The cargo approaches its destination.

Act V: Delivery (Contact → Footer)
The shipment arrives. A warehouse door opens. The journey completes.

Visual Design
• Background: Deep navy (#0A1628) gradient with subtle dawn orange at horizon line
• Ship Illustration: Detailed container vessel, 60% viewport width, positioned at bottom-right
• Water: Subtle animated wave pattern using CSS/SVG, low opacity
• Atmosphere: Faint orange glow at horizon suggesting dawn
• Container Highlight: One container (GNN-7749) subtly glows with Signal Blue outline
• Crane: Single gantry crane mid-operation, holding the featured container

Animation Behavior

On Load (before any scroll):
• Ship fades in from 0.8 scale to 1.0 over 1.5s, easing: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
• Crane arm completes a loading motion, placing container GNN-7749 onto the ship
• Water reflection shimmers to life
• Headline characters stagger in from bottom, 30ms delay per character
• Navigation slides down from top with 0.5s delay

While in Viewport (idle):
• Ship has subtle 2-4px vertical float (6s loop)
• Water ripples continuously (CSS animation)
• Crane arm makes micro-adjustments (8s loop)
• Particles: Faint mist/spray near water line

Scroll Indicator:
• Pulsing chevron or custom indicator: A miniature cargo container that bounces gently

Scroll Behavior

0% - 25% scroll through Hero:
• Crane releases container and retracts upward
• Ship begins slow rightward drift (parallax at 0.3x scroll speed)
• Horizon line stays fixed (parallax at 0x)
• Foreground dock elements drift left faster (parallax at 1.2x)

25% - 100% scroll through Hero:
• Ship accelerates slightly, moving toward right edge
• Water texture stretches horizontally suggesting movement
• Dawn gradient intensifies then fades into deep ocean night

Hover Interactions

Ship:
• Cursor changes to custom "voyage" cursor (compass icon)
• Subtle glow pulse on ship hull
• Tooltip appears near ship: "Container GNN-7749 departing Shanghai"

Primary CTA (Track Shipment):
• Background fills from left to right like a progress bar
• Icon transforms: static location pin becomes animated radar ping

Secondary CTA (Get a Quote):
• Border draws itself on hover (animated stroke)
• Subtle container icon slides into view

Required Imagery
Container ship illustration (side profile, high detail, shows 50+ containers)
Gantry crane illustration (operational pose)
Water texture (tileable, subtle)
Dawn sky gradient or photograph
Dock/port foreground elements (silhouettes)

Mobile Adaptations
• Ship scales to 90% viewport width, centered
• Headline stacks vertically, reduced to 40px
• Crane positioned above ship (vertical composition)
• CTAs stack vertically, full width
• Scroll indicator moves to center-bottom
• Parallax effects reduced by 50% (performance)
• Water animation simplified to CSS-only

Emotional Goal
Wonder + Anticipation

The user should feel they are witnessing the start of something significant. The dawn light, the massive scale of the ship, the precision of the crane—all communicate that this is a company that handles important cargo with care. The call to scroll feels like an invitation to follow the journey.

VIII. 10 Unique Premium Animation Ideas

These animations reinforce the logistics narrative and distinguish GNN's website from generic corporate sites:

The Persistent Ship
A small ship icon exists in a fixed position on screen (like a progress indicator) throughout the entire page. As the user scrolls, the ship progresses along a miniature route line. At certain sections, the ship transforms—becoming a crane (Fleet section), then a truck (Service Regions), then a delivered box (Contact). This creates a tangible sense of journey across the entire experience.

Container Content Morph
When service cards or content cards are revealed, they don't simply fade in—they emerge as shipping containers that rotate and "open" to reveal their content. The container door swings open, and the card content materializes inside. This transforms mundane content blocks into journey artifacts.

Wake Trail Typography
As the ship moves through sections, text in its path ripples subtly—as if disturbed by the ship's wake. When headlines appear near the ship's position, characters have a slight wave distortion that settles. This creates environmental awareness—the vehicle affects its surroundings.

Tidal Statistics
Instead of simple number counting, statistics animate like tidal flows. The number "rises" like water level, fills a container outline, and settles at the final value. For percentages, a circular container "fills" to the appropriate level. The metaphor: these numbers represent accumulated voyages, flowing in like the tide.

Crane Loading Transitions
Major section transitions feature a crane sequence. As one section ends, a crane arm swings in from the edge and "lifts" the section content. The arm swings out, carrying the content away, while the new section's content is "lowered" into place by a returning crane. This creates a deliberate, mechanical transition rhythm.

Route Drawing Cursor
When hovering over the interactive map, the cursor transforms into a small ship icon. As the user moves across the map, a temporary route line draws behind the cursor. Hovering over a port "docks" the cursor, and relevant route information appears. The user actively creates routes by exploring.

Cargo Manifest Form
The contact form doesn't look like a typical web form—it resembles a cargo manifest document. Fields are positioned like manifest line items. When filled, each field gets a simulated "stamp" or "seal" animation (like customs approval). The submit button is a "Ship It" action that initiates a container loading animation.

Weather Progression
As the user scrolls through the ocean journey sections, ambient weather changes. Starting with calm dawn in Hero, transitioning to gentle fog in Services, perhaps light rain in mid-journey, clearing skies as shore approaches, and finally sunny arrival in Contact. Weather particles drift across the viewport, creating atmospheric continuity.

Container Stacking Scroll
In long vertical content areas (like the Logistics Journey timeline), containers visually "stack" as the user scrolls. Each checkpoint reached adds a container to a stack visible at the edge of the screen. By section end, a full container stack shows progress. This gamifies scroll progress.

Delivery Door Reveal
The final testimonial or contact section begins with a warehouse door filling the viewport. As the user scrolls, the doors roll open (like actual warehouse rolling doors), revealing the content behind. The door opening is tied to scroll position—scroll down, doors open; scroll up, doors close. This creates a threshold moment: entering the "delivered" state.

IX. Implementation Notes for Cursor

Performance Priorities:
• Use CSS transforms and opacity for all animations (GPU acceleration)
• Limit SVG complexity on route lines (simplify paths on mobile)
• Implement scroll-driven animations with Intersection Observer, not scroll listeners
• Lazy load section assets; prioritize above-fold content
• Reduce particle counts on mobile by 70%

Animation Libraries Recommended:
• Framer Motion for React (timeline control, scroll-triggered animations)
• GSAP for complex sequences (crane operations, route drawing)
• Lottie for micro-animations (icons, status indicators)

Scroll Implementation:
• Use scroll snapping only on full-section transitions if tested for smoothness
• Implement scroll progress tracking at page level (for persistent ship)
• Debounce scroll handlers appropriately

Asset Strategy:

• Preload hero assets; defer below-fold
